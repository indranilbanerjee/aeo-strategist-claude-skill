## Priority Markets
1. **South Africa** — Most AI-mature; strongest digital infrastructure; English + Afrikaans dominant
2. **Nigeria** — Largest economy and population; Lagos fintech hub; fast mobile AI adoption
3. **Kenya** — East Africa hub; Nairobi startup ecosystem (Silicon Savannah); English strong
4. **Ghana** — West Africa emerging hub; English; growing startup scene
5. **Egypt** — Largest Arabic-speaking Africa market; significant tech investment

## AI Platform Landscape
- **Google Gemini**: Dominant across Africa (Google leads all major African markets)
- **ChatGPT**: Growing rapidly especially in Lagos, Nairobi, Johannesburg startup ecosystems
- **Meta AI**: Very high relevance — WhatsApp and Facebook penetration is among highest globally in Africa. Dark social AI through Meta is disproportionately influential.

## Africa AEO Notes

### Mobile-First is Non-Negotiable
Africa has some of the highest mobile-only internet usage globally. AEO content structure must be optimised for mobile retrieval. Voice queries in local languages are significant — Swahili, Yoruba, Igbo, Zulu, Afrikaans queries are growing but near-zero optimised.

### Fintech Hub Opportunity
Lagos (Nigeria) and Nairobi (Kenya) are global fintech hubs. AEO for African fintech brands targeting global investors and partners is an underdeveloped opportunity — English-language credibility content for these markets has outsized reach.

### WhatsApp as Primary Distribution
WhatsApp Business is the primary B2C communication channel across most African markets. Meta AI's integration with WhatsApp makes dark social AI especially important here. Brand name consistency and entity signals across the web help Meta AI's responses even without direct optimization.

### Trust Infrastructure Gap
Many African markets lack the established directory and regulatory portal ecosystem of India/US/EU. This means editorial media coverage and LinkedIn presence carry relatively higher weight as trust signals when directory listings are sparse.
