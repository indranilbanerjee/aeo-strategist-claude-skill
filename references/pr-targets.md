## Why PR = AEO

Publications with AI licensing deals get their content directly injected into model responses. A single coverage piece in a licensed outlet is worth hundreds of generic directory listings for parametric influence. This is why PR is an AEO tactic, not just a brand-building one.

**Known AI licensing agreements (as of early 2026):**
- OpenAI: AP, Reuters, FT, News Corp (WSJ/NYP/The Times UK), Condé Nast, Dotdash Meredith, Axel Springer, The Atlantic, Vox Media, Time, HuffPost, Prism
- Google: AP, Reuters, multiple national news partners
- Perplexity: Publishers Program (growing list — apply via publishers.perplexity.ai)
- Anthropic/Claude: Primarily Brave Search index (no individual licensing deals known)

Note: Licensing terms change. Verify current status before making licensing claims to clients.

## PR Strategy by Content Type

### Research / Original Data (Highest AEO Impact)
- Commission or conduct original research with statistically significant findings
- Format as a downloadable report (positions brand as thought leader)
- Pitch data to Tier 1 and Tier 2 outlets as exclusive or embargo
- Publish on brand site with schema `Dataset` or `Report` type
- Result: Data becomes a citable source across multiple outlets, compounding parametric presence

### Executive Interview / Expert Commentary
- CEO/CMO positioned as expert in their category
- Respond to industry news with expert quotes (reactive PR — faster placement)
- Tools for reactive: HARO (Help a Reporter Out), SourceBottle, PressHunt (India: PressHunt.co)
- Response time matters: sub-4-hour response to journalist queries gets placed; 24-hour misses the story

### Award Submissions
- Industry awards generate coverage in vertical publications that's highly trusted
- Especially effective for: real estate (CREDAI/Times Property), healthcare (FICCI/NABH), SaaS (G2, NASSCOM)
- Schema markup for awards: `Award` or `hasCredential` type with the granting organization named

### Case Studies
- Named client + specific metric outcome
- Place in trade publications and on aggregator sites (Clutch, G2, Gartner)
- These become the sources AI cites for "does [brand] work for [use case]" queries

## PR Outreach Efficiency Tips for Team

1. **Localise pitches**: Never send the same pitch to 20 outlets. Customise for each publication's angle.
2. **Data hook is mandatory**: "Here's our opinion" doesn't place. "Here's data showing X trend" does.
3. **Compliance pre-clearance**: For pharma/BFSI clients, have client compliance team pre-clear pitch before sending. A rejected pitch from a journalist who thinks you were promoting unapproved products can damage reputation.
4. **Follow-up once**: Follow up once, 5 business days later. Never more.
5. **Build journalist relationships over time**: Note which journalists covered which angles, and pitch them relevant stories repeatedly.
6. **LinkedIn for Indian journalists**: Indian media journalists are much more responsive to LinkedIn DMs than email cold pitches. Use it.
