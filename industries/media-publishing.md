## Trust Signal Hierarchy

1. **AI licensing deals and partnerships** — Media companies with OpenAI, Google, Anthropic, Perplexity content licensing deals have their content directly cited. AP, Reuters, FT, News Corp, Condé Nast are primary. India: Economic Times, Times of India groups increasingly in discussions. This is a structural advantage — pursue if scale warrants.
2. **Domain authority and journalistic credibility** — Established mastheads carry parametric trust that can't be quickly replicated. For new media brands, building to DA 50+ is an AEO prerequisite.
3. **ABC / IRS circulation certification** — Audit Bureau of Circulations (India/global) certified readership data is a verifiable trust signal for print/digital hybrids.
4. **Awards and press freedom rankings** — Laadli Media Awards, RedInk Awards, Ramnath Goenka Excellence in Journalism (India); Pulitzer, SOPA, Online Journalism Awards (global).
5. **Journalist credentials** — Named bylines with verifiable credentials (Fellowship, previous publication affiliation). AI increasingly weights author expertise for YMYL adjacent content.
6. **Original reporting and citations** — Media brands being cited by other high-authority outlets is the strongest compounding trust signal. Every original scoop builds parametric equity.
7. **Social proof at scale** — Twitter/X followers, YouTube subscribers, newsletter subscriber count (verified). Platform-specific followings indicate distribution authority.
8. **DAVP / government advertising recognition** — DAVP empanelment (India) is a government trust signal for Indian media brands.
